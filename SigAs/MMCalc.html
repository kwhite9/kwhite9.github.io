Introduction to Molar Mass

Welcome!

Today we will be learning about molar mass!
Molar mass is an important concept that chemists and chemistry students use every day.
When looking at a reaction, you need to know how many molecules (roughly) of each reactant you need, so that you know how much of each material you need to add to your reaction.
However, you can't count out each molecule!
We need to relate how many molecules you have to an easier to measure quantity. The easiest way to do this is with mass!
The way to relate mass and amount is through something called molar mass.

How Do I Find Molar Mass?

Molar mass is different for every compound, and must be calculated from your molecule's formula.
To do this, find the mass of all of the atoms in one molecule of your compound, and add them all together.
For example, ethane, C2H6, is composed of 2 carbons and 6 hydrogens. Carbon's molecular mass is 12 amu (atomic mass units), and hydrogen's molecular mass is 1 amu,
so ethanes molar mass would be (2x12)+(6x1)= 30 amu/molecule!

But Wait!

One last thing. The way we have this right now is actually the compounds molecular mass, not its molar mass.
The scales in lab aren't nearly precise enough to measure atomic mass units (1 atomic mass unit = 1 proton or neutron),
and if I'm weighing out grams of my compound, the amount of molecules is going to be ridiculously large.
To solve this problem, the last thing we need to learn about is the concept of moles.

Moles

What is a mole?
A mole is an amount, like a dozen. However, instead of 12, a mole is 6.022e23.
Moles can confuse students at first, but it works the exact same way a dozen does.
If I were to say "I have a dozen donuts", it means, "I have twelve donuts".
If I were to say "I have a mole of donuts", it means, "I have 622,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 donuts."
Moles are nice because they make the numbers much easier to manage ("1 mole" instead of "6.22e23 molecules")
But the mole is also a very specific number that relate atomic mass units to grams.
Because of this, the amount of atomic mass units per molecule of a substance is actually equal to the amount of grams per mole!

So, back to our earlier example, ethane's molecular mass is 30 atomic mass units per molecule, and it's molar mass is 30 grams per mole!
